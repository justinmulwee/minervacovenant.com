05

In the morning, Brick stared at the empty bag of coffee grounds, somehow disappointed that more didn't appear magically overnight. Still tired, but with nothing better to do, he returned to the ornate door at the end of its dark hallway, and knocked again. ON the second knock, there was a voice from within.

ClemenSamantha is still charging. It'll be a few minutes. BrickI'm not here for Samantha.

There were footsteps, then the door opened a crack, as far as the internal chain would allow. Clemen peeked through the crack.

What is it? I thought we'd settled everything. span We did. I want to see Heidi's room. Why? Are you serious?

Clemen only looked at him.

Brick do you want me to find her or not.

Clemen hesitated, closed the door, undid the chain and opened it fully.

It's up stairs. End of the hall. Could you.... please don't touch anything. Should she return, I want everything to be as she left it. Right.

I get it, thought Brick. The feeling that if you could return to a room unchanged, you could by some magic stop time. Reel it back. There was no wisdom in it. But he understood it.

06

The room was smaller than he expected, but neat and luxurious. Light gray walls with red curtains. Everything was orderly. There was a plain desk with a display and a keyboard connected to a phone. On the other side was an oval mirror mounted to the wall. There was a bed and a closet.

The desk had one long drawer. It's full of makeup and little else. Quite a variety here. For example, there were four different foundations in slightly different colors. Most items here had only been slightly used, perhaps once or twice.

The walls are clean except for a large, framed photo. It depicts a brightly lit cityscape at night, full of people. You recognize it immediately as the city of New Alexandria.

The city feels close by when watching videos on the internet, but there is something about the motionless, printed photo that reminds you of its distance. Memories float in, one after another. You bat them away.

A neatly made, queen sized bed.

The mattress is a bit worn in on the right side, and not at all on the left.

The left side is clean. On the right side, there are a couple of long, auburn hairs on one of the pillows. Judging from her photo, these were Heidi's. No, wait. One is auburn, the other is blonde.

Nothing of interest but hanging clothes. A simple wardrobe of neutral colors. White shirts, black pants, a black cocktail dress. Several pairs of shoes for varying occasions. Like the rest of the room, everything here suggests a minimal but elegant personal style.
An ordinary folding tablet, like everyone carries. It's locked, presumably requiring Heidi's fingerprint.

07

Vaguely aware of movement from the corner of his eye, he looked to the door to see Samantha standing there, in the doorway of the room across the hall, looking at him.

Done for the moment, Brick left the room and walked passed Samantha. "Good morning," he said flatly. She said nothing.

She looked at him for a moment, then closed her eyes and pulled a heavy cord out from the back of her neck and let it fall to the floor, before proceeding down the hall, and down the stairs.

Brick glanced around the room. He supposed he was finished for now. He disconnected the phone. He followed her down the stairs. She took her place beside Clemen. He wondered if she was going to report every detail of his work at every opportunity, but she said nothing.

BrickI'm guessing you haven't been able to get into this?

Here Brick held up the phone. Clemen shook his head.

I'm taking it.

Clemen opened his mouth, then closed it again. and nodded.

Alright then, I'l let you know what I find.

Clemen looked at Samantha and gave a nod. She stepped forward and did her curtsy again.

SamanthaI look forward to accompanying you.

With that, the two left together, leaving Clemen standing alone among his artworks.
