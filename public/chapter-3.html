Distance

01

Two months ago, there was a knock at the door of the clinic. Brick peeked through the hole to see three people: a big Russian guy. A tall, skinny woman who was obviously trying to suppress a smile. And a serious looking guy with a military haircut. Brick opened the door. It was the serious looking guy who spoke.

BurnsideAccording to official records... it's your birthday.

They looked about the room as they entered.

MityaNice place. BurnsideYou get much business? Brick Not at first, but it's picking up. My customers are... a bit slow to trust humans. BurnsideSmart of them, isn't it. BrickMaybe. BurnsideAnyway, look what I found.

He held up a tall glass bottle.

BrickThat's not some hooch you made in your basement, is it? BurnsideNo, no, no. The real deal. Some fool must have imported it, and then left without it. MityaImagine paying for shipping out here, and then not drinking it.

Brick examined the bottle. Already opened, with approximately two, no, three shots missing.

BrickAlready been in it, I see. Mitya smiled innocently. MityaHad to make sure the goods were up to our standard. BrickBrick uh huh.

02

Brick got some glasses from a cupboard, and poured them all a drink. He took a sip himself, and closed his eyes to appreciate it.

Brick So what have you lot been up to?

They looked at each other, a hint of awkwardness hung in the air.

MityaSorry, can't share that with civilians. LottieDon't say it like *that*.

Brick simply nodded. Burnside shrugged.

BurnsideSame as always. Making sure the human race doesn't go extinct or anything. Brick Yeah? How's that looking? BurnsideBetter, since you took out our main problem. Nice shot by the way, like, damn. MityaYeah, about that. We've been trying to settle something. who's the best shot in Caldera. You or Lottie. Lottiewhat are you, twelve? MityaMitya come on. like, who would win in a gun fight? Burnside, rolling his eyesThose aren't even the same question BrickHmm. it depends whether Lottie *knows* we're going to have a gunfight. MityaSuch a *you* answer. BurnsideI can tell you we're looking into something. If you run into any skinless androids, steer cleer. BrickSkinless? BurnsideThere's word some androids are removing all their skin as a sign of separation with humanity. Or some such nonsense. They resent the fact that they look human. It's time to move beyond all that, they say. MityaOf course, if they want to rock the Terminator look, we ain't the fashion police. BurnsideTrue. but it's just the kind of rhetoric we've been worried about. So we'll see where it goes. that's all.

03

BurnsideHave you seen they made a little shrine? the androids, I mean.

Brick was just starting to smile, but at this his expression fell.

Brick ... yeah. Yeah, I've seen it. MityaWeird. It's like, the mind of an android isn't something we were meant to understand, you know? LottieI don't know, I don't think it's that hard to understand. It's like a memorial for a parent, right?

04

BurnsideI heard you're leaving.

He looked at his empty glass, but didn't pour another.

BurnsideI was gonna ask if you were ready to join up with us again, but I guess that answers my question. If you wanted your job back, you could have it in a heartbeat. Not that I care, but Lottie missies you. Lottie Well, I didn't say *that.* BurnsideI didn't say you said it. BurnsideYou know, I want to go home too, but we came for a reason. On that note. we should get back to work.

He stood up. Lottie followed suit. Mitya finished his drink first. They headed toward the door.

BurnsideI know you want to be some kind of neutral party here, but there's no such thing. This is too important.

Lottie lingered, looking around the room.

LottieThis little clinic really is a nice place. I'm glad you... I'm glad you found a place.

In a moment, they were gone. He looked at the bottle. He felt heavy. It was a nice parting gift.
